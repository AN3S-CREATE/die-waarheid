# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect all contributors, maintainers, and participants to uphold this Code of Conduct in all interactions within the Die Waarheid project.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Treat all participants with courtesy and respect
- **Being inclusive** - Welcome people of all backgrounds and experiences
- **Being constructive** - Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Being professional** - Maintain professional communication standards
- **Being collaborative** - Work together towards common goals
- **Being honest** - Communicate truthfully and transparently

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, identity, race, religion, or disability
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

### Reporting Issues

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers. Reports should include:

- Description of the incident
- People involved
- When and where it occurred
- Any relevant context

All reports will be treated confidentially and investigated promptly.

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning** - First-time minor violations
2. **Temporary Ban** - Repeated violations or serious misconduct
3. **Permanent Ban** - Severe or repeated violations

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Official project events
- Public spaces when representing the project
- Private communications related to project business
